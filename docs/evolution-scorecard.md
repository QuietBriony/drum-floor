# Evolution Scorecard

Use this scorecard after listening to a generated candidate.

Score from `1` to `5`.

- `1`: actively hurts the groove
- `2`: weak or distracting
- `3`: usable but not special
- `4`: strong and musical
- `5`: keeper

## Required score axes

- `pocket`: does the whole bar sit in the right place?
- `space`: does it leave enough air for the band?
- `bass_lock`: does kick behavior support bass without over-doubling?
- `ghost_glue`: do ghost notes connect the feel without clutter?
- `snare_lag_feel`: does late snare feel intentional rather than sloppy?
- `fill_naturalness`: are fills rare, contextual, and playable?
- `mix_weight`: do kick, snare, hat, crash, and room hints point in a useful direction?
- `surprise`: does it have small human decisions without random chaos?
- `repeatability`: would this still work across several phrases?

## Required notes

Each score should include short text notes:

- `what_worked`
- `what_failed`
- `next_hint`

These notes are used by a future `suggest-evolution` command, but they do not modify pattern frames automatically.

## Listening targets

Valid listening targets:

- `browser`
- `ableton`
- `ep133_preview`

`ep133_preview` means previewing the routing idea. It does not mean the CLI touched EP-133.
